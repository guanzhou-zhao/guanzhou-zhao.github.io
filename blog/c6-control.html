When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

When programming, that a problem is not solved after trying all sorts of method is a trigger of anxiety. What can trigger anger is like the code reviewer put out unreasonable advices or a task is given verbally and unclearly that leads to time wasting. Or requirements keep changing not because they know a new way, but because they don't know any way and keep trying.

Not sure.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

As I had learned to control my emotions, I already did Stop, and Response in a more proper way. But this is kind of like suppresing on emotions and by doing stop and response in a different way just doesn't cause bad result, I don't feel good myself.

Siberian North Railroad exercise is a way to look the trigger and response process in higher resolution and give me more space to choose. Stop, Breathe and Notice let me see emotions objectively and they are just experiences. Through reflection I know the source of the trigger, understand the history, I can think in other's shoes and see positively. Be friend with emotions, let them be there and don't feed them. Response in humor and positive way.
